Shear Design Theory — Members with shear and flexure only (NSCP 2015 §422.5)

Strength reduction & nominal shear

NSCP 2015 §421.2.1 — strength reduction factor for one-way shear (ACI 318-19 §21.2.1).

$$\phi = 0.75 \;\;\text{(NSCP 2010 / 2015)} \qquad \phi = 0.85 \;\;\text{(NSCP 2001 — deprecated)}$$

$$V_n = V_c + V_s \qquad V_u = \phi \, V_n$$

\(V_u\) is the factored shear at the section being checked, \(V_n\) is the nominal shear strength of the member. \(V_n\) splits between the concrete (\(V_c\)) and the transverse reinforcement (\(V_s\)).

Critical section — \(V_u\) at \(d\) or at the face?

NSCP 2015 §409.4.3 — when the critical section may be taken at \(d\) from the face of the support.

Take \(V_u\) at distance \(d\) from the face of the support when all three conditions are met:

  1. Support reactions introduce compression into the end regions of the member.
  2. Loads are applied at or near the top of the member.
  3. No concentrated load is applied between the face of the support and distance \(d\).

$$V_u = \frac{w_u\,(\ell_n - 2d)}{2} \;=\; R - w_u\,d$$

If any condition fails (planted column, tension-hanging load, etc.) — take \(V_u\) at the face of the support, no reduction.

Concrete shear strength \(V_c\)

NSCP 2015 §422.5.5.1 — non-prestressed members, no axial load.

$$V_c = 0.17 \, \lambda \, \sqrt{f'_c} \, b_w \, d$$

\(0.17 \, \lambda \, \sqrt{f'_c}\) is the concrete shear-stress strength; \(b_w \, d\) is the effective concrete area at the cracked section. \(\lambda\) is the lightweight-concrete modification factor: 1.0 normal-weight, 0.85 sand-LW, 0.75 all-LW (§419.2.4).

One-way shear strength of transverse reinforcement \(V_s\)

NSCP 2015 §422.5.10.5.3 — vertical stirrups perpendicular to the longitudinal axis.

$$V_s = \frac{A_v \, f_{yt} \, d}{s} \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad s \le \frac{A_v \, f_{yt} \, d}{V_s}$$

\(A_v\) is the cross-sectional area of all stirrup legs in one cross-section, \(f_{yt}\) is the yield strength of the transverse reinforcement (capped at 420 MPa by §422.5.10.2), \(s\) is the centre-to-centre spacing.

Minimum stirrup area \(A_{v,\min}\)

NSCP 2015 §409.6.3.3 — minimum shear reinforcement.

$$A_{v,\min} = 0.35 \, \frac{b_w \, s}{f_{yt}} \qquad \text{use if } f'_c < 31.87 \; \text{MPa}$$

$$A_{v,\min} = 0.062 \, \sqrt{f'_c} \, \frac{b_w \, s}{f_{yt}} \qquad \text{use if } f'_c \ge 31.87 \; \text{MPa}$$

The crossover \(f'_c = 31.87\) MPa is where \(0.062\sqrt{f'_c} = 0.35\) (i.e. \(f'_c = (0.35/0.062)^2\)). Take the larger of the two expressions in code.

Maximum spacing from \(A_{v,\min}\)

$$s \le \frac{A_v \, f_{yt}}{0.35 \, b_w} \qquad \text{use if } f'_c < 31.87 \; \text{MPa}$$

$$s \le \frac{A_v \, f_{yt}}{0.062 \, \sqrt{f'_c} \, b_w} \qquad \text{use if } f'_c \ge 31.87 \; \text{MPa}$$

These are the algebraic inverse of the \(A_{v,\min}\) expressions above — given the actual \(A_v\) you've selected, this is the largest spacing that still satisfies the minimum-steel rule.

Maximum spacing from \(V_s\) vs \(0.33\sqrt{f'_c}\,b_w\,d\)

NSCP 2015 §409.7.6.2.2 — spacing limits depend on \(V_s\).

Case A — \(V_s \le 0.33\sqrt{f'_c}\,b_w\,d\)

$$s \le \frac{d}{2} \qquad \text{and} \qquad s \le 600 \; \text{mm}$$

Case B — \(V_s > 0.33\sqrt{f'_c}\,b_w\,d\)

$$s \le \frac{d}{4} \qquad \text{and} \qquad s \le 300 \; \text{mm}$$

When stirrups are not required

NSCP 2015 §409.6.3.1 — \(A_{v,\min}\) and \(s_{\max}\) not required.

$$V_u \le \tfrac{1}{2} \, \phi \, V_c$$

Example. \(V_u = 50\) kN, \(V_c = 350\) kN, \(\phi V_c = 0.75 \times 350 = 263\) kN, \(\tfrac{1}{2}\phi V_c = 131\) kN. Since \(V_u = 50 < 131\) kN, the concrete alone is more than enough — no stirrups needed (provide minimum per good practice anyway).

Beam dimensions not adequate

NSCP 2015 §422.5.1.2 — upper bound on \(V_s\).

$$V_s > 0.66 \, \sqrt{f'_c} \, b_w \, d$$

Even at the maximum shear strength the stirrups can provide, the section is not economical or adequate to resist the demand. Increase \(b\) or \(h\).

Summary of the design procedure

Apply in order:

  1. Solve for the support reactions \(R\).
  2. Determine \(V_u\) — at \(d\) from the face if the three conditions are satisfied, else at the face.
  3. Determine \(V_c\), \(\phi V_c\), and \(\tfrac{1}{2}\phi V_c\).
  4. Determine \(0.33\sqrt{f'_c}\,b_w\,d\) and \(0.66\sqrt{f'_c}\,b_w\,d\); compute \(V_s = V_u/\phi - V_c\).
  5. Compare \(\phi V_c\) and \(\tfrac{1}{2}\phi V_c\) with \(V_u\) — classifies the case (none / minimum / designed).
  6. Compare \(0.33\sqrt{f'_c}\,b_w\,d\) and \(0.66\sqrt{f'_c}\,b_w\,d\) with \(V_s\) — picks the spacing rule (d/2 or d/4) and confirms the section is not overloaded.
  7. Solve for the maximum spacing \(s\) or the minimum area \(A_v\).
